About Me
A California Central Coast native, I received my Bachelor of Science in Mathematics with a minor in Classics from UC Santa Barbara in 2019. At the end of my two year stint at Yardi Systems in Goleta, CA as a Technical Account Manager for a diverse set of business clients, frequently working with large databases and SQL for their property management product left me yearning to dive deeper into the data world. In June of 2021, I left Yardi to pursue a Master's degree in Statistics at CSU Fullerton and received my degree in May of 2023. While attending CSUF, I also began working part-time at the Southern California Coastal Water Research Project (SCCWRP) as a Programming Assistant, helping develop data tools, pipelines, and dashboards for scientists and other interal stakeholders alike.
My education at CSUF provided me with both theory and applications of statistical thinking on topics including exploratory data analysis, visualization, experimental design, and statistical modeling. Some of the modeling techniques I learned include:
  • generalized linear regression
  • tree-based methods like random forests
  • neural networks
  • Bayesian inference
Of course, there will always be more to learn in the dynamic and fast-paced field of data, so I am prepared and excited to continue learning!
At SCCWRP, I was fortunate to work with a variety of tools and frameworks such as:
  • Microsoft PowerBI - dashboards
  • Microsoft Azure - cloud data piplines
  • Python Flask - general web apps
  • Python Pandas - data processing/cleaning
  • Docker - application deployment
  • R Shiny - dashboards
  • R tidyverse - data processing/cleaning
When I'm not at work, you can find me engrossed in a fantasy novel, on the edge of my seat at the movies, playing (video- or board-) games with friends, or moshing at the local heavy metal show!
